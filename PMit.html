It/its pronouns indicates that a person prefers if others refer to it by those when talking about it. Pronouns do not indicate a person's gender and it can be harmful to assume gender or pronouns based on appearance. Given that Jordan uses it/its pronouns, instead of saying "Jordan went to the park," someone could say "it went to the park." Some people may feel uncomfortable reffering to someone as "it," but it is wrong to use other pronouns if you were already made aware of it's pronouns.
