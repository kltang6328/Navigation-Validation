She/her/hers pronouns indicates that a person prefers if others refer to her by those when talking about her. Pronouns do not indicate a person's gender and it can be harmful to assume gender or pronouns based on appearance. Given that Gray uses she/her pronouns, instead of saying "Gray had to leave early," someone could say "she had to leave early."
